Meet Our Board of Trustees

Our Board of Trustees is composed of dedicated professionals who bring a wealth of experience and expertise to ADS Nyanza. They are committed to guiding our organization towards achieving its mission of community empowerment and sustainable development. Learn more about each of our esteemed board members below.

The Rt. Rev’d Canon Joshua Owiti Ouma

Member of the Board of Trustees and Non Executive Member of the Board

The Rt. Rev’d Canon Joshua Owiti Ouma

Currently serving as the Diocesan Bishop of the Anglican Diocese of Maseno East, he holds a Bachelor of Divinity from Uganda Christian University and a Licentiate in Theology from St. Paul’s University. He has undertaken specialized training in resource mobilization, guidance and counseling, youth leadership, substance abuse prevention, and HIV/AIDS awareness, among others. As an ordained minister, he has provided dedicated parochial oversight as a Parish Priest and rose to the esteemed position of Provost—a key church role that deputizes the Diocesan Bishop in ecclesiastical responsibilities and chairs the Liturgical Committee. Beyond his pastoral duties, he has held several leadership positions, including Chairman of the National Council of Churches of Kenya (NCCK) Kisumu Region, Board Member of Community Initiatives Support Services, Chairman of the Strategic Building and Development Committee at St. Stephen’s Cathedral in the See of Maseno South, and Chairman of the Board of Mission

The Rt. Rev’d Prof. David Hellington Kodia

Member of the Board of Trustees

The Rt. Rev’d Prof. David Hellington Kodia

Prof. Kodia holds a Ph.D. (Summa Cum Laude) from Northwestern Christian University, along with a Master’s and Bachelor’s in Theology from Charles University in Prague. A prolific author, he has published extensively on topics including theology, sociology, truth in modern society, the role of the church in democratization, gendered socio-economic disparities, poverty, volunteerism, and biblical justice. His works include The Sociology, Theology, and Intricacies of Truth in Modern Society, Religious, Cultural, and Social Burdens as Major Contributors to Poverty in Africa, The Role of the Church in Democratization Processes in Kenya, Balancing a Gendered Worldview: Politics and Religion in Focus, and Theology and Politics of Biblical Exposition and its Relevance to Church and Society, among others. Currently, he serves as the Diocesan Bishop of the Anglican Diocese of Bondo. He has previously been the Acting Vice-Chancellor of Great Lakes University of Kisumu (GLUK), Principal of Bishop Okullu College, Registrar of GLUK, Director of Studies at Carlile College, and Principal of St. Philips Bible College. His consultancy work spans Ghana and South Sudan in areas of strategic planning, peace, and conflict resolution. Bishop Kodia has held various governance roles within the Anglican Church of Kenya, including Clerical Secretary to the Provincial Synod, member of the Communication and Research Committee, Secretary of the Health and HIV/AIDS Committee, and Anglican Colleges Representative at the Provincial Synod. He also served on the Permanent Commission for the Subdivision of Anglican Dioceses and has been collated and installed as a Canon Theologian.

The Rt. Rev’d Charles Ochieng Ong’injo

Member of the Board of Trustees

The Rt. Rev’d Charles Ochieng Ong’injo - Member of the Board of Trustees

Bishop Charles holds a Master of Arts in Religion from Maseno University and a Bachelor of Divinity from St. Paul’s University. He is the Diocesan Bishop of the Anglican Diocese of Maseno South and has previously served as the Vicar General of the Diocese, along with roles as Archdeacon, Rural Dean, and Vicar responsible for pastoral duties. Additionally, he is a Theological Education by Extension Trainer. His leadership extends beyond the church, having served as the Vice Chairman of the Kisumu County Education Board, a member of the Kisumu West Technical Committee on HIV/AIDS, and a member of the Probation and Aftercare Committee. He has also contributed to the Court Users' Committee at Maseno Law Courts and served as a Presiding Officer for the Electoral Commission of Kenya. Bishop Charles has been actively involved in various Boards of Management, including for Maseno Girls Boarding, Chulaimbo Secondary School, Wagwer Secondary School, and Maseno School for the Deaf. He led the establishment of All Saints Academy Primary School, a church venture focused on modeling students using the Gospel. He also chaired Global Faith Partnership, an initiative for Orphans and Vulnerable Children (OVC), offering feeding programs to help students concentrate on their studies.
