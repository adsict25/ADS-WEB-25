Program Overview

The Global Fund Project in Homa Bay County is dedicated to reducing HIV transmission through targeted initiatives such as Fisherfolk empowerment, PMTCT for mothers, and innovative defaulter tracing mechanisms. These efforts aim to enhance health outcomes and empower the community.

Success Stories in Homa Bay

Fisherfolk Empowered to Combat HIV

Posted on: 1st January 2025

The Global Fund initiative has significantly empowered fisherfolk communities in Homa Bay by providing essential resources and raising awareness, leading to a substantial reduction in HIV transmission and improved overall health outcomes.

Improved PMTCT Outcomes

Posted on: 12th January 2025

Through the PMTCT program supported by the Global Fund, mothers in Homa Bay have achieved higher rates of preventing mother-to-child HIV transmission, ensuring healthier futures for their children. This initiative has provided critical support and education, empowering mothers to protect their babies from HIV.

Defaulter Tracking Saves Lives

Posted on: 20th January 2025

With innovative defaulter tracking mechanisms, the Global Fund project in Homa Bay has reconnected hundreds of patients to essential HIV treatment, improving adherence and health outcomes.

Volunteer Spotlight

Adhiambo Achieng

Volunteer in Caregiver Training Programs

Adhiambo's efforts in training caregivers have led to better care for over 200 children. His workshops have equipped caregivers with essential skills and knowledge, resulting in a 30% improvement in child care practices. Akoth Onyango

Volunteer in Health and Nutrition Programs

Akoth's work in health and nutrition has significantly improved the well-being of many families. Her innovative programs have not only reduced malnutrition rates by 30% but also provided specialized healthcare for children below the age of 3 years, ensuring a healthier start to life for the youngest members of the community.
