A Teen Caregiver’s Journey in Moments that Matter Training

In the serene village of Homa Bay, 17-year-old Elizabeth Moraa is gaining valuable knowledge in Early Childhood Development (ECD) to better support the children in her care.

“Every morning, I wake up knowing that I’m gaining the skills to make a difference in a child’s life. These kids deserve the best, and I’m learning how to give them that.”

Elizabeth Moraa

Currently undergoing training, Elizabeth is learning how to engage children with interactive activities that promote their proper development. Her dedication has made her an example for many parents, highlighting the importance of investing in ECD for young children.

The Moments that Matter project has significantly impacted Elizabeth's life by providing her with the skills and knowledge needed to support early childhood development. This initiative has empowered her to create a nurturing environment for the children in her care, fostering their growth and development.

Evelyne Atieno: A Mother’s Love and a Green Thumb

Balancing the roles of mother, caregiver, and entrepreneur, Evelyne Atieno has turned a small vegetable garden into a thriving source of food security and income for her family.

“I wanted to take care of my children and provide for them at the same time. Vegetable planting has become a source of both income and nourishment for us.”

Evelyne Atieno

Through her garden, Evelyne not only ensures healthy meals for her family but also teaches young children about food sustainability. Her story is a beacon of hope for caregivers looking to blend innovation with nurturing.

Local Impact, Global Lessons

The stories of Elizabeth and Evelyne highlight the resilience and ingenuity of caregivers in rural communities. Whether through education or sustainable agriculture, they are shaping the future of Homa Bay, one child at a time.

The Moments that Matter (MtM) project emphasizes the importance of nurturing care and early childhood development. By providing training and resources, MtM empowers caregivers to create supportive environments where children can thrive and reach their full potential.
