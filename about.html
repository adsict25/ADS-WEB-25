ADS Nyanza History

ADS Nyanza traces its roots back to 1975 when the Anglican Church of Kenya (ACK) Diocese of Maseno South launched the Integrated Rural Development Program (IRDP). This pioneering initiative aimed to promote holistic community well-being by focusing on key areas such as community health, food production, appropriate technology, and education.

In 1981, the Church of the Province of Kenya (CPK) established the Provincial Board of Christian Community Services (PBCCS), which laid the groundwork for a church-led development agenda. By May 1983, PBCCS organized a consultative meeting to expand the church’s ministry, ensuring that its reach encompassed both rural and urban poor communities.

This led to the establishment of the Christian Community Services (CCS), which became the church's dedicated development arm. CCS took on the dual role of fostering program development and delivering essential services, while raising critical awareness about societal issues affecting communities.

Recognizing the need for more integrated and collaborative approaches, the various Diocesan Development Departments merged into broader regional platforms. This culminated in the formation of Inter-Diocesan Christian Community Services (IDCCS) in 1997, a milestone that united development efforts across multiple dioceses.

In 2010, IDCCS underwent a rebranding process, emerging as ADS Nyanza, a name that reflects its commitment to sustainable development and community transformation. Over the decades, ADS Nyanza has consistently championed transformational human development, advocating for equity in the sharing of God’s abundant resources. Through innovative programs and strategic partnerships, the organization continues to empower communities, improve livelihoods, and build a sustainable future for all.

Our Impact

Our Approach

At ADS Nyanza, we focus on empowering communities to achieve independence, self-sufficiency, and dignity. Our bespoke programs are tailored to the unique cultural, economic, political, and environmental contexts of each community.

What We Do

Through innovative approaches, we create lasting impact in the communities we serve. With our tailored interventions, we empower individuals and groups, leveraging proven methodologies to drive sustainable change.

Where We Work

We work across Nyanza regions in Kenya, reaching over 10,000 communities with transformative and impactful solutions that empower individuals, foster sustainable development, and create lasting positive change.
