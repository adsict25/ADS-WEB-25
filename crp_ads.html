Climate Resilient Project

Improved Livelihood of Vulnerable Households

Program Overview

This project aims to improve the livelihoods of vulnerable households in Siaya, Kisumu, Homa Bay, Migori, and Nyamira counties. By strengthening community structures, promoting financial resilience through SWE groups, enhancing agricultural productivity, and improving access to quality healthcare, the program seeks to achieve sustainable income growth and better health outcomes by December 2024.

Testimonies of Change

Explore the inspiring journey of Judith Anyango, whose life has been transformed by our projects.

Judith Anyango, Rakwaro, Homa Bay

Judith, a widow and mother of five, faced immense struggles after losing her husband. Relying on her small hairdressing business, she barely earned KSH 200 a day. Floods damaged her home, compounding her challenges. Through ADS Nyanza's Integrated Climate Resilience Project, Judith joined Savings With Education groups and became a community volunteer. She expanded her hairdressing business and cassava farming, now earning KSH 700 per day. Judith adopted climate-smart practices, leading to healthy crops and bountiful harvests. She now enjoys a balanced diet, sells extra produce, and supports her children's education.

"Being chosen and trained made me feel worth much more,"

Judith's journey of resilience and transformation continued as she adopted climate-smart practices and expanded her business. Her success story inspired many in her community, showcasing the impact of ADS Nyanza's initiatives.

"Today, I am assured of not only what to eat, but of a balanced diet and selling extra produce for clothes and school fees making my family life much more lovely. May the Church be blessed!"

A Story of Transformation

Discover the transformative journey of Brigit Mboga and her husband Enoch Omiso, who have greatly benefited from our projects.

Brigit Mboga and Enoch Omiso, Timi, South Nyamira

Brigit, aged 19, and her husband Enoch, aged 24, faced financial struggles before joining ADS Nyanza's Integrated Climate Resilience Project. Through their participation in Savings With Education groups, they learned poultry farming and climate resilience practices.

"The training has been a blessing, and through hard work and savings, we were able to start our poultry farm. Now we’re able to support our family." – Brigit

Brigit and Enoch's journey took a transformative turn after selling their first batch of 60 hens. With the money earned, they purchased a motorbike for transport, supporting their savings and improving their quality of life.

"The project has shown us that, with the right knowledge and faith, we can build a sustainable future for our family." – Enoch

Success Stories from Climate Resilience Project (CRP)

SUCCESS STORY OF DOROTHY ADHIAMBO

Location: Chulaimbo village, Kisumu County, Kenya

Background: Dorothy is a 40-year-old mother of four children, passionate about agriculture and committed to improving her family’s livelihood. Her journey with the Climate Resilience Project (CRP) started with challenges in planting and nutrition.

"Before this project, I was behind in knowledge and skills. Now, I know how to prepare my seedbed and transplant seedlings. This has increased my crop yield and improved my diet!"

Impact: Dorothy now grows vegetables for both consumption and selling, easing the financial burden and improving her family's health. Her success has inspired her community to adopt kitchen gardens and balanced diets.

Lesson Learned: The project helped Dorothy build resilience by teaching her sustainable farming practices and nutrition education. She continues to teach others in her community.

SUCCESS STORY OF JACULLINE

Location: Another village, Kisumu County, Kenya

Background: Jaculline is a 35-year-old mother of three children, dedicated to sustainable farming and improving her family's nutrition. Her involvement with the Climate Resilience Project (CRP) began with similar challenges in agriculture and diet.

"The training I received has transformed my approach to farming. I now have a thriving kitchen garden that supports my family's needs and provides extra income."

Impact: Jaculline's kitchen garden has not only improved her family's diet but also provided a source of income. Her success story has motivated others in her village to start their own gardens.

Lesson Learned: Through the project, Jaculline learned valuable skills in sustainable farming and nutrition, which she now shares with her community.
