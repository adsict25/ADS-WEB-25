Board of Trustees

Meet Our Board of Directors

Our Board of Directors is composed of dedicated professionals who bring a wealth of experience and expertise to ADS Nyanza. They are committed to guiding our organization towards achieving its mission of community empowerment and sustainable development. Learn more about each of our esteemed board members below.

The Rt. Rev’d Charles Ochieng Ong’injo

Board Chairperson

The Rt. Rev’d Charles Ochieng Ong’injo

Bishop Charles, Chairperson of the ADS Nyanza Board of Directors, is a seasoned leader with a Master of Arts in Religion from Maseno University and a Bachelor of Divinity from St. Paul’s University. As the Diocesan Bishop of Maseno South, he has provided strategic leadership in governance, education, and community development. At ADS Nyanza, his leadership has strengthened partnerships, expanded outreach programs, and enhanced service delivery in livelihoods, health, and social empowerment. He has been instrumental in advancing initiatives for vulnerable communities, including education support, economic empowerment, and faith-driven development. His governance oversight extends to multiple educational and community boards, ensuring impactful and sustainable programs.

The Rev'd CPA Benard Enos Otieno Owuor

Honorary Treasurer, Finance & Admin

The Rev'd CPA Benard Enos Otieno Owuor

The Rev'd CPA Benard Enos Otieno Owuor serves as the Honorary Treasurer, Finance & Administration at ADS Nyanza, bringing a unique blend of financial expertise and faith-driven leadership. As a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) and an ordained minister, he ensures the organization’s financial integrity while aligning resources with its mission of social transformation. In his role, he oversees financial planning, budgeting, and reporting, ensuring transparency and accountability in all fiscal matters. He provides strategic guidance on resource mobilization, grant management, and compliance with financial policies. His leadership supports the efficient administration of ADS Nyanza’s programs, strengthening financial sustainability and operational effectiveness..

Mrs Nelly Atieno Otieno

Programs Chairlady

Mrs Nelly Atieno Otieno - Programs Chairlady

Mrs. Nelly Atieno Otieno serves as the Programs Chair on the ADS Nyanza Board of Directors, providing strategic oversight and leadership in program development and implementation. With a strong commitment to community empowerment, she ensures that ADS Nyanza’s initiatives align with its mission to improve livelihoods, health, and social well-being. In her role, she guides program design, monitoring, and impact evaluation, ensuring efficiency and sustainability. Her leadership fosters partnerships, resource mobilization, and innovation in service delivery, driving meaningful change in the communities ADS Nyanza serves.

The Rt. Rev’d Dr. Canon Joshua Owiti Ouma
Non Executive Member of the Board

The Rt. Rev’d Dr. Canon Joshua Owiti Ouma

The Rt. Rev’d Dr. Canon Joshua Owiti Ouma serves as a Member of the Board of Trustees and a Non-Executive Member of the Board at ADS Nyanza. He brings a wealth of experience in governance, resource mobilization, and strategic development. Currently, he is the Diocesan Bishop of the Anglican Diocese of Maseno East and holds a Bachelor of Divinity from Uganda Christian University and a Licentiate in Theology from St. Paul’s University. With extensive training in resource mobilization, guidance and counseling, youth leadership, substance abuse, and HIV/AIDS, he has provided impactful leadership in both ecclesiastical and community service. As a former Provost and Chairman of the Liturgical Committee, he played a key role in diocesan administration. His leadership extends to chairing NCCK Kisumu Region, the Board of Mission, and the Strategic Building and Development Committee at St. Stephen’s Cathedral, alongside serving as a Board Member for Community Initiatives Support Services. At ADS Nyanza, he contributes to strategic decision-making, governance, and mission-driven development, ensuring impactful service delivery across communities.

Mr. Samwel Omondi Ochieng

Executive Director

Mr. Samwel Omondi Ochieng

Mr. Samwel Omondi Ochieng is the Executive Director & Secretary to the Board at ADS Nyanza, leading strategic growth, governance, and program implementation. With over 20 years of experience in community health, strategy development, and resource mobilization, he drives impactful initiatives that enhance livelihoods and social empowerment. He holds a Master’s in Community Health and Development (Great Lakes University of Kisumu), a Bachelor’s in Sociology (University of Nairobi), and a Higher Diploma in Community Health. He has served on multiple boards, including the Anglican Church of Kenya’s Social Services Board and Kenya Community-Based Health Financing, and has chaired several School Boards of Management. A former Hospital Administrator, Gender Coordinator, and Program Manager, he has led major community interventions. As a researcher and lecturer, he has published on food security and contributed to policy discussions. At ADS Nyanza, he champions organizational development, strategic partnerships, and financial sustainability, ensuring long-term impact in the communities served.

FCS Jophece Yogo

Secretary to the Board

FCS Jophece Yogo

Jophece Yogo is an experienced and dedicated professional, serving as the Secretary to the Board. With a strong background in corporate governance and administrative excellence, Jophece plays a critical role in supporting the Board’s operations, ensuring that meetings, documentation, and decision-making processes are efficient and compliant with organizational standards. As Secretary to the Board, Jophece is responsible for facilitating communication between the Board and stakeholders, maintaining records of Board meetings, and providing legal and procedural guidance. Her meticulous attention to detail and organizational skills have been instrumental in maintaining smooth governance and ensuring that the Board’s objectives align with the organization's mission and vision.
