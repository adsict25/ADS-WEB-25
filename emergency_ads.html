ADS-Nyanza’s Impact on Combating Drought in Suba South

As the region faces recurring droughts that threaten food security and water availability, ADS-Nyanza has taken a proactive approach to mitigate the effects. The emergency response efforts focus on providing farmers with critical information on water conservation and drought-resistant crops, ensuring food security even in the harshest conditions.

ADS-Nyanza’s Drought Response Strategies

The organization has mobilized local communities in Suba South to adopt climate-smart agricultural techniques, use water-efficient technologies, and create drought-resilient food systems.

  • Water Harvesting Systems: Construction of water pans and dams to collect and store rainwater for dry periods.
  • Drought-Resistant Crops: Encouraging the cultivation of drought-tolerant varieties of crops like millet, sorghum, and cassava.
  • Awareness Campaigns: Providing education on soil management and conservation to reduce the impact of prolonged dry spells.

Community Impact

Since the program’s inception, more than 1,000 farmers have benefitted from the adoption of drought-resistant crops, leading to a 30% increase in crop yields in Suba South.

Looking Ahead

ADS-Nyanza plans to expand the drought resilience efforts by including new irrigation systems and exploring sustainable water sources for long-term impact.

Tree Planting Campaign in Kisumu County: Combating Soil Erosion and Flooding

In a bid to combat the devastating impacts of floods and soil erosion, ADS-Nyanza organized a tree planting campaign in Kisumu County, targeting the high-risk areas of Ahero and Nyando. This initiative aimed at restoring degraded land, reducing flood risks, and promoting environmental sustainability in the region.

ADS-Nyanza’s Role in Flood Prevention

The floods in Kisumu County have caused massive soil erosion, affecting agriculture and livelihoods. ADS-Nyanza’s staff, in collaboration with local communities, has planted over 10,000 trees across the region to stabilize soil and reduce the intensity of floodwaters.

  • Tree Planting for Soil Stabilization: The planted trees are critical for preventing further erosion and flooding.
  • Community Engagement: Local schools and farmers were involved in planting and maintaining the trees, ensuring long-term sustainability.
  • Awareness Programs: ADS-Nyanza has also been conducting training on environmental conservation and flood management techniques.

Tree Planting Campaign

Impact on the Environment

The tree planting initiative has helped reduce soil erosion by over 40% in areas like Ahero and Nyando, significantly lowering the risk of flooding in the upcoming rainy seasons.

Community Involvement

The success of the tree planting campaign is largely attributed to the active participation of local communities, schools, and farmers who have taken ownership of the initiative and are committed to maintaining the planted trees.

Note: The following locations in Nyanza are vulnerable to extreme weather conditions like floods and droughts, necessitating the need for swift disaster response initiatives:
  • Suba South & Suba North (Migori County)
  • Nyatike (Migori County)
  • Homa Bay County (Kendu Bay & Rachuonyo North)
  • Kisumu County (Nyando, Ahero, Kisumu East)
  • Kisii County (Gucha & Bobasi)
