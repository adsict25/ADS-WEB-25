About the Program

We collaborate closely with communities and vulnerable households with children aged 0–3 years to promote nurturing care for early childhood development. Our focus is on fostering positive parenting practices, promoting social behavior change, and encouraging male involvement in caregiving roles.

We empower primary caregivers with the knowledge and resources needed to support their children’s holistic development—ensuring that young children not only survive but thrive during these critical years.

Additionally, we partner with faith leaders and local institutions to enhance psychosocial support for caregivers and advocate for policies that prioritize early childhood development.

Program Highlights

Innovative tools and approaches that shape nurturing care and early learning environments.

The Babahood Manual

A guide promoting positive fatherhood and male engagement in early childcare—building stronger family bonds.

Community Circles

Regular caregiver discussion groups led by trained facilitators to share experiences and nurture peer support.

Faith & Learning Partnership

Collaborating with faith leaders to integrate nurturing care messages into community outreach and teaching.

Where We Work

The Moments That Matter (MTM) program operates across Homa Bay County and neighboring regions, reaching families in remote and underserved areas. Through collaborations with community health volunteers, faith leaders, and early learning centers, we create nurturing environments for children aged 0–3 years.

Our footprint continues to expand as we work with partners to strengthen early childhood systems and community support structures.

Success Stories

Stories of transformation and hope from MTM communities.

Play to Potential

Caregivers adopted play-based learning, boosting child participation by 40%.

In Nyakach, caregivers introduced structured play-based learning with MTM facilitators. Attendance rose and caregivers reported stronger engagement and bonding through playful learning.

Literacy Boost

Parents used home storytelling to improve literacy skills among toddlers.

Home-based storytelling circles trained parents to support children’s early literacy. Within months, caregivers reported improved word recognition and confidence in learning.

Caregiver Champions

Faith leaders mobilized support circles to improve mental wellbeing.

Faith leaders became champions of nurturing care, promoting psychosocial wellbeing and strengthening community cohesion through peer mentorship and advocacy.
